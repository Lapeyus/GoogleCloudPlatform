Understanding SLOs


Contrary to popular belief, SLOs aren't set at the maximum performance a service can achieve. Rather, the business will determine an appropriate  and achievable  level of the performance or reliability that the provider can realistically deliver.

Service Level Objectives (SLOs) are Not Set at Maximum Performance


Contrary to popular belief, SLOs are not set at the maximum performance a service can achieve. Instead, businesses determine an appropriate and achievable level of performance or reliability that the provider can realistically deliver.


Financial Viability and Realistic Expectations


The service must be financially viable, so it cannot be made too responsive or too available, as this would be too expensive. SLOs are not immutable and may change as the service is continually improved and upgraded.


Four Golden Monitoring Signals


When creating SLOS, consider the four golden monitoring signals: latency, traffic, errors, and resource saturation. It's essential to be realistic and not over optimistic when designing SLOs.


Error Budget Methodology


Many organizations use an error budget methodology for maintenance and managing service windows. This approach assumes that every service will not run perfectly and provides acceptable error limits for the SLOS. Each service is given a set level of credit, which is a set amount of errors measured in downtime, considered acceptable (normal and expected maintenance and service downtime).


Service Requirements


A key design focus is on the requirements important to stakeholders, such as business goals. Requirements are measurable, like the requirement for high responsiveness to users, contained in an objective, e.g., the system must respond to customer requests in no more than 400 milliseconds latency.


Measurable Service Indicators


Design objectives can be mapped to corresponding measurable service indicators, such as measuring round trip time between receiving a user request and delivery of a result to the gateway.

Topic 1: Service Performance and Reliability


This topic is centered around the concept of Service Level Objectives (SLOs) and their relationship with service performance and reliability. It discusses how SLOs are not set at maximum performance levels, but rather at achievable levels that balance business needs with financial viability.


Topic 2: Error Budgeting and Maintenance


This topic focuses on the use of error budgets as a maintenance strategy for services. It explains how error budgets provide a framework for acceptable errors and downtime, allowing services to be maintained within a budgeted limit while minimizing service outages.


Topic 3: Design Requirements and Objectives


This topic emphasizes the importance of design requirements and objectives in defining service performance. It highlights the need to map operational requirements to measurable service indicators, such as latency and round trip time, to ensure that services meet stakeholder needs.

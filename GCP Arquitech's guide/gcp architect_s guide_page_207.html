Understanding Cloud Service Design


The process of designing a cloud service involves multiple tiers: business logic, storage data layer, security layer, reliability and availability, and project design constraints.


Business Logic and Storage Data Layer


The first tier is the business logic, which defines the requirements for the storage data layer. This understanding will determine the type of database to use, such as NoSQL or global relational databases. The storage data layer should be designed in conjunction with the business logic.


Security Layer


The fourth and final tier is the security layer, which requires thorough scrutiny before deployment. Security controls and threat surfaces must be considered throughout the design process, including identity and access management (IAM). Bastion hosts, service accounts, access-lists, and firewall rules can be used to bolster vulnerable areas in the design.


Reliability and Availability


Once the business logic and storage data layer are designed, the focus shifts to reliability and availability. This involves considering how resilient the design is in case of failures, its ability to scale above forecasted loads, and how it will cope with unexpected popularity.


Project Design Constraints


Despite the ideal design, real-world constraints such as budget limitations must be considered. Compromises may need to be made to balance optimal design with economic realities. Forecasts and planning for load and capacity are still necessary, even in the face of budget constraints.

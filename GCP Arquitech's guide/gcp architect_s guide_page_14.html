Google Cloud Platform Overview


Google provides a vast pool of resources that are allocated dynamically to clients on a pay-as-you-use basis, allowing for efficient resource allocation across its demand-driven network of customers.


Key Benefits


    

  • Dynamic Resource Allocation: Resources such as CPU, memory, network bandwidth, and storage can be allocated seamlessly and rapidly without human intervention.
    • 

  • Scalability: Demand-driven scaling means that resources can be scaled up or down according to changing needs.
    • 

  • Cost-Effective: Clients only pay for the resources they use, reducing operational spending and overall costs.
    • 



Security


Google's cloud infrastructure is highly secure due to its own Titan security chips and secure-boot processes. The company also supports a wide array of vendor software and hardware, ensuring that clients have access to a broad range of security solutions.


Economies of Scale


Google takes advantage of economies of scale by buying and building infrastructure, licenses, and services in bulk, passing the savings on to customers. Its largest partner network provides access to a wide range of open-source and proprietary software, pre-provisioned and available at a click.


Ease of Use


The Google Cloud Platform offers considerable ease-of-use advantages when provisioning servers and application stacks or environments. Clients can provision LAMP (Linux, Apache, MySQL, and PHP) stacks and other environments in just a click, with correct versions, dependencies, and libraries installed automatically. Networking is also configured automatically, eliminating the need for in-house expert-level networking skills.


GCP Charging Model


The Google Cloud Platform's pay-as-you-use model offers several ways to reduce operational spending and overall costs. For start-ups and Greenfield companies, cloud computing reduces capital expenditure required for servers and network equipment. Established businesses can also benefit from reduced maintenance, administration, and operational costs of running a data room/centre.

Cloud Service Design


Overview


Cloud service design is a critical component of cloud architecture, and this chapter introduces the layered and iterative approach to designing cloud services.


State


State refers to the current status of a system or application. In cloud service design, state is essential for understanding the current state of a system and identifying areas for improvement.


Measurement


Measurement involves collecting data on the performance and behavior of a system or application. This data can be used to identify trends, optimize performance, and make informed decisions about future development.


Requirements


Requirements are the needs and expectations of users, stakeholders, and other parties involved in the cloud service design process. These requirements must be understood and addressed throughout the design process to ensure that the final product meets the needs of all parties.


Cloud Service Design Process


The cloud service design process is iterative and layered, involving multiple stages and feedback loops. This approach allows designers to refine their designs over time, incorporating new information and insights as they become available.

Choosing the Right Platform for Containerized Applications


When it comes to deploying containerized applications, developers have several options to consider. The choice of platform depends on their specific needs and expertise.


App Engine Standard Environment


If developers don't need to use Docker containers in their application, App Engine standard environment might be a better option. In this case, they can simply deploy their code, and App Engine will handle the infrastructure for them.


App Engine Flexible Environment


On the other hand, there is also App Engine -Flexible environment, which is a developer-focused, no-ops compute infrastructure platform that abstracts the underlying infrastructure to some extent in favor of developer friendliness. This platform runs Docker containers in VMs, making it easy to deploy and handle operations such as logging, scaling, versioned upgrades, and traffic splitting.


Kubernetes Engine (GKE)


However, if developers are familiar with Docker, containers, configuration, and settings, and want to run containerized workloads, Kubernetes Engine will be a better option. GKE provides more control but also requires an understanding of the underlying infrastructure and container orchestration concepts. It is particularly suitable for developers already running Kubernetes on premises, as it can manage both on-premises and cloud environments across a VPN.


Choosing the Right Platform


In summary, the choice of platform depends on the developer's expertise and specific needs. App Engine standard environment is suitable for simple deployments, while App Engine Flexible Environment provides more flexibility and control. Kubernetes Engine is ideal for developers with experience in container orchestration and management.

Topic 1


Storage Capacity Management
The text discusses the need for adequate storage capacity in a system, specifically mentioning the limitations of using SSD drives with 22 Bigtable servers, which would not be sufficient to meet the forecasted double growth.


Topic 2


Operational Manageability
Designing a service that prioritizes operational manageability is crucial, as it involves considering continuous improvement and deployment, monitoring and alerting, and incident response. This approach ensures the system can adapt to changing conditions and market demands.


Topic 3


Knowledge Transfer and Handover
After deploying a new system, it's essential to transfer knowledge of its characteristics and operational best practices to the people who will operate and maintain it. This process involves stabilizing the system and performing a handover to ensure smooth operation in production.


Topic 4


Continuous Improvement and Evolution
A well-designed service should be adaptable and evolve over time to meet changing market or production conditions. Regularly reviewing service indicators and objectives helps identify when the original design needs revision, ensuring the system remains relevant and effective.

The expected growth in data storage needs for the Bigtable servers is 5.5Tb per SSD.


2. Why are HDD drives considered more appropriate than SSD drives for this application?


HDD drives are considered more appropriate because their larger capacity would be more suitable for the application, which is not latency or disk throughput sensitive.


3. What is the purpose of a design team's knowledge transfer to operations after deployment?


The purpose of transferring knowledge from the design team to operations is to ensure that the system is stabilized and maintained properly, and that its operational characteristics are understood by those who will be operating it.


Analytical Questions


1. How does the concept of SLO (Service Level Objective) and SLI (Service Level Indicator) contribute to managing a newly deployed system?


SLO and SLI provide an objective method for managing a newly deployed system, helping to keep it running in production and meeting its operational objectives.


2. What is the significance of iteratively reviewing service indicators and objectives in system design?


Iterative review of service indicators and objectives helps identify when the production environment and original design assumptions no longer meet market or production conditions, prompting a revisit of the design to evolve the system.


3. How does continuous improvement and deployment relate to operational manageability in system design?


Continuous improvement and deployment are key aspects of operational manageability, as they enable the system to adapt to changing needs and ensure its ongoing stability and performance.


Application Questions


1. What would be a potential consequence of not considering operational manageability in system design?


Not considering operational manageability could lead to difficulties in stabilizing and maintaining the system after deployment, potentially resulting in downtime or decreased performance.


2. How might the concept of continuous improvement and deployment apply to real-world scenarios beyond Bigtable servers?


Continuous improvement and deployment can be applied to various systems and applications, including software development, infrastructure management, and other areas where adaptability and responsiveness are crucial.


3. What role do monitoring and alerting play in ensuring operational manageability?


Monitoring and alerting are essential components of operational manageability, as they enable the detection of potential issues before they become major problems, allowing for swift action to be taken to mitigate their impact.
